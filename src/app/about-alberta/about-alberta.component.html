Alberta is the westernmost of Canada’s three Prairie provinces. Often known as Canada's "energy province", Alberta is home to over 4 million people and is growing quickly. The bulk of this population resides in Alberta’s two largest cities: Edmonton, which serves as the provincial capital, and Calgary.

Alberta is a global leader in the oil and gas industry, significantly contributing to Canada’s energy sector with its abundant oil sands, natural gas, and coal resources that attract international investments. Beyond energy, Alberta has a diverse and growing industrial and service economy, which includes strong agricultural production of cattle, wheat, and other crops, as well as sectors like technology and tourism.

In addition to its robust economy, Alberta is recognized for its breathtaking natural landscapes. The Rocky Mountains, with their stunning peaks and pristine lakes, offer numerous outdoor recreational activities, including hiking, skiing, and wildlife viewing. National parks like Banff and Jasper are not only popular tourist destinations but also crucial for preserving the province's rich biodiversity.

Alberta's economic growth has made it a top destination for immigration to Canada, with a favorable job market and high quality of life attracting individuals and families from diverse backgrounds. This influx contributes to the province's rich cultural tapestry, celebrated through numerous festivals and events, including the Calgary Stampede, which highlights its cowboy heritage, and various cultural festivals in Edmonton.

Map of Alberta

Alberta is located in the western part of Canada, bordered by British Columbia to the west, Saskatchewan to the east, and the United States to the south.

Flag of Alberta

The flag of Alberta, adopted in 1968, features the provincial shield of arms on a blue background. The shield itself includes a red cross of St. George, representing British heritage, as well as a representation of Alberta’s diverse geography, with golden fields symbolizing agriculture and the Rocky Mountains highlighting the province's stunning natural landscapes.
